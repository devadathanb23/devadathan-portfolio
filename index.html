Process

Logic meets
emotion.

I don't design pretty screens. I design decisions — ones grounded in research, shaped by systems thinking, and built for the human on the other side.

01 Empathize

Interviews, observations, secondary research. I spend disproportionate time here. Most teams don't.

02 Define

A problem statement specific enough to design for, open enough to explore. The hardest step to get right.

03 Ideate

Broadly first, then pruned ruthlessly. I prefer 50 bad ideas over 3 polished ones that went untested.

04 Prototype

Low-fi first, always. Speed of learning matters more than fidelity at this stage.

05 Test

Real work in front of real people. Every assumption is a hypothesis until proven otherwise.

Notice Finding the real problem before anyone else has framed it. The issue is rarely where it first appears.
Simplify Reducing complexity without losing depth. The best UX is invisible — it never makes users feel like they're working.
Shape Translating insight into screens, flows, and systems that hold together under real-world pressure.

About

I lead with
questions.

Based in Kerala. Four years studying design. Six months working on real briefs for real clients. The gap between those two things is where I actually learned to design.

My instinct is always to go deeper than the brief — to ask why the problem exists before I attempt to solve it. I'm drawn to overlooked systems. The ones everyone uses and nobody's thought to redesign.

I'm not naturally artistic. I chose design because of the thinking, not the drawing. I learn fast when I'm doing, slow when I'm reading. I'll give everything I have to a company that gives me real problems to work on.
